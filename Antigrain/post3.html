Experiment: The economics of cauliflower rice

Sep 09, 2013

;

I did an experiment the other day. Using some store bought cauliflower and a good ol' fashioned cheese grater, I wanted to actually quantify how much it sucks to prepare a batch of cauliflower of rice from scratch.

Here we go: first, the cost breakdown: I purchased 3 heads of cauliflower from my local Jewel Osco.

  • Price: $1.49/lb
  • Total weight: 8.5 lbs
  • Total cost: $12.70

After washing trimming and cutting the cauliflower heads, I started grating. And grating. And grating. In total, I spent an hour grating the cauliflower and bagging it up. It sucked a lot. On top of that, what I had to show for my effort was quite a bit less than what I started with. The final weight of the 4.5 bags I had was about 2.8lbs or 33% of the starting weightâ€”(the majority of the weight in a cauliflower is in the core: the leafy green part on the outside leads to a super dense white core). Unfortunately most of what you pay for at the grocery store goes unused.

That 2.8lbs equated to about 8.2 cups of fresh rice (155g/cup). Considering the ratio of fresh to freeze-dried is about 15:1, the yield is equivalent to about 3 of the sized bags I am thinking about for Anti-Grain.

$12.70 and an hour later I had about 3 bags worth of rice. Hopefully our first product will soon relieve people from this torture.
