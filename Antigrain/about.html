About Anti-Grain

The story of Anti-Grain Foods, the people behind it, and some helpful tips and tricks for using our flours in your recipes.

Vibrant alternatives for Grain-Haters*

We're not just taking the grain out of food--we won't make anything unless we can replace it with something healthy, substantial, and beneficial to your body. That's a promise.

*There are a lot of reasons to hate grains--we call it the Grain-Hating Spectrum. So whether you're;
paleo, grain-free, wheat-free, gluten-free, allergen-free, wholegrarian, or just trying to cut back on grains

We're not going to leave you hanging:

We experiment with our products constantly and tap the knowlege of the biggest & baddest Grain-Haters out there to keep you inspired and empowered no matter where you fall on the Grain-Hating spectrum.

Usage Tips

Just like this site: We make everything from scratch to learn as much as we can. Through thorough experimentation. The below are some guidelines and observations we’ve noticed while using our flours. The macronutrient breakdowns are accurate and do not include moisture (which is why the percentages don’t add up to 100). We are still experimenting, and still learning. If you find something new different in your kitchen please reach out and let us know. All our flours are made from a single ingredient that has been drum-dried at relatively low temperatures to retain as many nutrients as possible.

Squash Flour:

Our Squash flour is the most versatile of the bunch. It's natural flavor is present without overpowering the finished dish.

Flavor Think subtle zucchini bread or pumpkin pie. Lends well in both sweet and savory dishes.
Baking Bakes nicely in quickbreads and works well in noodles. Leavens well with yeast and both baking soda and baking powder.
Usage Mixes great with nut flours, rice flours and other Anti-Grain flours. May use as a 1-to-1 substitute for tapioca starch or potato starch (in most dishes).
Comments Lends great moisture and holds it well. Might have to add a bit of extra moisture if you’re making a dough or batter.
Starch: 62% Protein: 4% Fiber: 11% Fat: 2% Sugar: 11%
