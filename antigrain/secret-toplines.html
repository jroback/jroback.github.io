Top Secret Toplines (on the Future of the Flour Market)

June 18, 2014

;

Food Business

r
  1. 2013 Sales of Natural Flour and Baking Mixes totaled $300MM
  2. 73% of those sales were in the conventional channel
  3. Natural Market grew approx 20% over 2012
  4. Flours alone represent about $115MM or 38% of the category (avg of both channels)
  5. Flours Grew 17% YOY
  6. Bob's Red Mill Almond flour 16oz. is the fastest growing SKU in the space (growing 120% YOY to $9.6MM in 2013 conventional channel, and 70% YOY to $3.8MM in the natural channel)
  7. Bob's Red Mill Almond Flour also grew 100% YOU in 2013 (to 1.38 MM) suggesting Paleo consumers could be driving a significant portion of that growth.
  8. Bob's Red Mill and King Arthur make up about 50% of the category (27% and 20% respectively) Rest of the space is fragmented
  9. All flours and Baking Mixes (All brands all channels) was worth $3.6B in 2011 Meaning that natural products make up about 10% of the industry
  10. Price per ounce in the Natural Channel flour and bread mix space ranges between $.20 - $.67 per ounce. Gluten free flours in the $.50 per ounce range

Summary:

If we connect the dots here, it seems like the gluten-free flours, especially nut flours, are driving the growth in the category. The fact that a single SKU of Almond flour (Bob's Red Mill 16oz) was worth over $9 Million last year and witnessed triple-digit growth, makes me feel even more promise for the introduction of new, Paleo-friendly flours.
